Methodology

The Overall Blockchain Adaptability Score measures how well-positioned a country is for blockchain adoption. Each of the three dimensions is weighted equally at ~33.3%, reflecting the view that no single factor—market demand, legal clarity, or network infrastructure—can drive sustainable blockchain adoption on its own.

Overall = (Economic × 0.333) + (Regulatory × 0.333) + (Technical × 0.333)

Why equal weights? Economic adoption without regulatory clarity creates legal risk. Regulatory clarity without infrastructure means no network to regulate. Infrastructure without adoption means unused capacity. All three pillars must be present for a country to be truly blockchain-ready.

1. Economic Adoption Score (Version F)

Quantifies real-world blockchain economic penetration using seven normalized, weighted indicators. Version F decomposes the Chainalysis composite index into separate CEX and DeFi sub-indexes. View full methodology breakdown →

Economic = (CEX Activity × 0.15) + (DeFi × 0.10) + (Ownership × 0.25) + (Remittances × 0.15) + (Currency Instability × 0.10) + (CEX/GDP × 0.15) + (Internet × 0.10)
  • Crypto Ownership % × 0.25 — Population penetration rate from Triple-A data.
  • CEX Activity × 0.15 — Average of Chainalysis Retail CEX and Institutional CEX sub-index ranks (PPP-weighted, inverted).
  • CEX Volume / GDP × 0.15 — CoinGecko 24h exchange volume divided by national GDP, log-transformed.
  • Remittances per Capita × 0.15 — World Bank remittance inflows divided by population.
  • DeFi Activity × 0.10 — Chainalysis DeFi value received sub-index rank (PPP-weighted, inverted).
  • Currency Instability × 0.10 — Composite of log(inflation), 5-year depreciation vs USD, and Heritage capital controls.
  • Internet Penetration × 0.10 — Infrastructure gating variable.
2. Regulatory Clarity Index

Evaluates how clearly a country has defined its legal framework for blockchain and crypto. Six dimensions are each rated on a 1–3 scale and weighted equally, then normalized to a 0–1 range:

Regulatory = (Stablecoin × 0.167) + (Licensing × 0.167) + (Tax × 0.167) + (Banking × 0.167) + (Enforcement × 0.167) + (DeFi × 0.167)

Why equal weights? Each sub-dimension captures a distinct aspect of regulatory maturity. All six must work together to form a coherent regulatory environment. Ratings: 3 = clear, codified rules; 2 = partial or in-progress; 1 = absent, banned, or no guidance.

  • Stablecoin Law: Legislation governing stablecoin issuance, reserves, and usage
  • Licensing Framework: Whether exchanges and service providers must obtain licenses
  • Tax Clarity: Defined tax treatment for crypto gains, income, and transactions
  • Banking Access: Whether crypto businesses can access traditional banking
  • Enforcement Stance: Active enforcement of crypto regulations
  • DeFi Recognition: Whether DeFi protocols are acknowledged within the legal framework

Tier thresholds:

  • Comprehensive (0.83–1.0) · Developing (0.61–0.82) · Ambiguous (0.40–0.60) · Hostile (<0.40)
3. Technical Infrastructure Score (Version 2)

Measures each country's blockchain network footprint using five continuous, per-capita pillars aggregated with a weighted geometric mean (non-compensatory). All node/LN metrics are normalized per 100k internet users — not raw population — to fairly compare countries of different sizes. Near-zero performance on any pillar pulls the composite toward zero. View full data table →

Technical = BTC_Score0.25 × ETH_Score0.25 × Mining_Score0.15 × LN_Score0.20 × Enabling_Score0.15

Each pillar score = min(1, ln(raw_per_100k + 1) / ln(ceiling + 1)), yielding a continuous 0–1 value. Mining uses global hashrate % directly. Enabling Environment is a composite of internet penetration, electricity access, and average internet speed.

  • BTC Nodes (w=0.25): Reachable BTC nodes per 100k internet users — ceiling 1.1/100k. Source: Bitnodes.io
  • ETH Nodes (w=0.25): Reachable ETH nodes per 100k internet users — ceiling 1.2/100k. Source: Ethernodes.org
  • Mining Hashrate (w=0.15): Country share of global BTC hashrate, log-normalized — ceiling 40%. Source: Hashrate Index
  • Lightning Network (w=0.20): LN nodes per 100k internet users — ceiling 1.5/100k. Source: mempool.emzy.de
  • Enabling Environment (w=0.15): Composite of internet penetration (40%), electricity access (30%), avg internet speed (30%). Sources: ITU, World Bank, Speedtest Global Index

Tier thresholds (v2):

  • Network Leader (≥0.70) · Advanced (0.45–0.69) · Developing (0.20–0.44) · Emerging (0.08–0.19) · Nascent (<0.08)

Key v2 findings: India drops dramatically (0.060) due to its 759M internet users base making per-capita node density tiny despite decent absolute counts. UK ranks Advanced (0.470) not Major Hub — near-zero mining (0.05% hashrate) penalizes it under geometric mean. Ethiopia drops from Emerging to Nascent: its 2.6% mining share cannot compensate for near-zero nodes in every other pillar.
