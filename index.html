What we measure

Three pillars, weighted equally.

Any one pillar alone is misleading. Adoption without rules is risky for users; rules without adoption is dead law; infrastructure without users is unused capacity. The overall score is the average of all three.

The headline finding

The relationship between regulation and crypto adoption is conditional, not uniform.

The same restrictive law produces opposite outcomes in different countries depending on the economic conditions underneath it. That single observation, drawn from comparing the regulatory and economic scores across all 20 countries, is what drives every other finding in this project.

In stable economies
Restriction suppresses adoption.
In distressed economies
Restriction accelerates independent adoption.

If you take only one thing from this site, take that: regulation alone does not predict adoption. Necessity does most of the work, and policy reacts to it.

Selected discoveries

Four numbers worth remembering.

Every claim on the site is grounded in the data. Here are the most striking single-number findings.

FINDING 03

Japan: best regulation, almost no adoption.

Highest regulatory clarity in the index (0.944) yet 18th of 20 in economic adoption (0.205). The single sharpest piece of evidence that comprehensive regulation does not guarantee users.

FINDING 05

Four countries control 58.6% of Bitcoin mining.

The US (37.5%), Russia (16.4%), Ethiopia (2.6%), and Indonesia (2.1%) together produce nearly 60% of global hashrate. Crypto’s most decentralized layer is geographically concentrated.

Read more →
FINDING 02

Every top-5 adopter has a distress driver.

Vietnam, Ukraine, Philippines, US, Pakistan: each one is in the top five for a different reason (currency stress, conflict, remittances, market scale). The US is the only stable economy in the group.

Read more →
FINDING 06

DeFi is unregulated everywhere.

Across all 20 countries in the index, no country scores a 3 (full recognition) on DeFi, including the five ‘Comprehensive’ regulators. Stablecoin and DeFi rules are the global blind spot.

About this project

Why this index exists.

Most reports on crypto adoption rank countries on a single composite number. That hides the mechanism. A country can land near the top of an aggregate ranking because of speculative trading, because of remittances, because it has cheap mining electricity, or because its laws happen to be welcoming, and those four stories require completely different responses from policymakers, businesses, and users.

This index splits the score into three pillars on purpose so the underlying driver of each country’s position is legible. The findings page then ties those pillars back together to argue something specific: that regulation only behaves the way most policymakers expect it to when it is read against the economic conditions underneath it.
