Methodology

The Overall Blockchain Adaptability Score measures how well-positioned a country is for blockchain adoption by averaging three equally-weighted dimensions:

Overall Score = (Economic Score + Regulatory Score + Technical Score) / 3

1. Economic Adoption Score

Quantifies real-world blockchain economic penetration using four normalized, weighted indicators:

Economic = (Ownership × 0.30) + (Chainalysis Rank × 0.30) + (Volume × 0.20) + (Remittances × 0.20)

2. Regulatory Clarity Index

Evaluates how clearly a country has defined its legal framework for blockchain and crypto. Six dimensions are each rated 1–3:

Regulatory = (Stablecoin Law + Licensing + Tax Clarity + Banking Access + Enforcement + DeFi Recognition) / 18
  • Comprehensive (0.83–1.0): Clear, multi-faceted regulatory framework
  • Developing (0.61–0.82): Active regulation in progress
  • Ambiguous (0.40–0.60): Minimal or unclear rules
  • Hostile (<0.40): Bans or no recognition

3. Technical Infrastructure Score

Measures the physical blockchain network presence within a country. Four dimensions are each rated 1–3:

Technical = (BTC Nodes + ETH Nodes + Mining Hashrate + Lightning/Validators) / 12
  • Major Hub (0.90–1.0): Top-tier node count and mining presence
  • Strong (0.75–0.89): Significant infrastructure
  • Moderate (0.58–0.74): Notable but limited presence
  • Emerging (0.42–0.57): Early-stage infrastructure
  • Minimal (<0.42): Negligible network footprint

Scores range from 0 to 1, where higher values indicate greater blockchain adaptability. The overall score equally weights all three dimensions, recognizing that true blockchain readiness requires economic demand, legal clarity, and network infrastructure working together. Only 20 countries were selected based on data availability and regional diversity.
